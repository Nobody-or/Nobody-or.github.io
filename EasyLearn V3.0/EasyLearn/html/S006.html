Maze(BFS)

Description


The Dragon God feels bored and comes to the dungeon. It is a huge maze, with some passable roads, some impassable walls, and some monsters. Although the Dragon God can easily kill these monsters, he thinks this is too boring.

He observes that these monsters will disappear every k seconds (for example, when k=3, the monsters in the 3rd, 6th, 9th, ... second Is disappearing).

Every second, the Dragon God can choose to walk up, down, left and right (can't stay still).

The Dragon God wants to know the shortest time required to reach the exit under the condition of avoiding all monsters.

Input


Enter an integer T (1≤T≤10) in the first line, which represents the number of testing case groups.

The first line of each group of testing cases includes three integers n, m (1≤n, m≤100) and k (1≤k≤50) respectively representing the number of rows, columns of the dungeon maze and the disappearance intervals of the monsters.

The next n lines represent the maze, . Represents the passable road, # represents the wall, * represents the monster, S represents the starting point, and E represents the exit.

Output

Output an integer, which represents the shortest time for the Dragon God to walk out of the dungeon maze.

If the Dragon God can't get out of the dungeon, output -1.

Sample Input

1
6 6 2
...S..
...#..
.#....
...#.#
...*..
..*E*.

Sample Output

7
